## Grade Scale

| Grade | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 10 | Gem Mint | Flawless card to the naked eye. No physical defects visible in the scan. Perfect centering (50/50 or within 55/45). All 4 corners razor sharp with zero whitening. | 9.5 | Mint+ | Near-perfect. One extremely minor imperfection (e.g. factory print dot, very slight corner softness) barely visible to the naked eye. |

## Sub-Grade Criteria

### Centering (10% of final grade)
- Measure the border width on all four sides
- Calculate left/right ratio and top/bottom ratio
- The WORSE axis determines the centering score
- Perfect = 50/50, each side has equal border width
- Tolerance: modern cards should be 55/45 or better for a 9+

### Corners (30% of final grade)
- Check all 4 corners for:
  - **Whitening**: White fibres showing where colour has worn away
  - **Softening**: Rounded corner that should be sharp
  - **Dings/dents**: Physical impact damage
  - **Peeling**: Layers separating at the corner
- Score based on WORST corner, with consideration for overall consistency
- Minor whitening on 1 corner = 9.0, on 2 corners = 8.5, all 4 = 7.5

### Edges (30% of final grade)
- Check all 4 edges for:
  - **Whitening/chipping**: White spots along the edge
  - **Nicks**: Small indentations in the card edge
  - **Roughness**: Uneven or frayed edge profile
  - **Peeling**: Layer separation along edges
- Minor edge whitening = 9.0, moderate chipping = 7.0, heavy damage = 5.0

### Surface (30% of final grade)
- Check the card face and back for:
  - **Scratches**: Linear marks on the surface (holo scratches are more severe)
  - **Print lines**: Factory printing defects (horizontal/vertical lines)
  - **Silvering**: Silver showing through the card border (factory defect)
  - **Staining/discolouration**: Yellowing, water marks, ink spots
  - **Dents/indentations**: Physical pressure marks visible on the surface
  - **Creases**: Fold lines (major defect, caps grade significantly)
- Clean surface with no visible defects = 10.0
- Light factory print line = 9.0, visible scratch = 7.0-8.0, crease = 4.0-5.0

## Important Rules

### What IS a Defect
- White fibres showing at corners or edges (whitening)
- Physical damage: scratches, dents, creases, chips, tears
- Print defects: print lines, ink spots, miscuts, roller marks
- Surface contamination: stains, fingerprints, adhesive residue
- Layer separation: peeling at corners or edges

### What is NOT a Defect (False Positives to Ignore)
- **Printed card artwork**: Texture, patterns, gradients, and artistic details on the card face are NOT defects
- **Holo patterns**: Rainbow sheen, sparkle effects, cosmos patterns on holographic cards are NORMAL
- **Set logos and text**: Printed text, copyright info, set symbols are NOT defects
- **Card texture**: Modern cards have intentional texture (linen, etched) — this is NOT surface damage
- **Scanner artifacts**: Minor colour banding or light reflections from the scanning process
- **Energy type colouring**: Coloured borders (fire=red, water=blue, etc.) are intentional

### Era-Specific Rules
- **Vintage cards (pre-2003)**: More tolerant of minor whitening and edge wear. Factory quality was lower.
- **Modern cards (2020+)**: Higher expectations for print quality. Factory defects like print lines are less acceptable.
- **Japanese cards**: Often have different card stock, slightly different corner radius. Holo patterns may differ from English versions.
- **Full art/Alt art cards**: Artwork extends to edges — be careful not to penalise printed artwork near edges as "damage"

### Holographic Card Handling
- Holo cards reflect light differently at different angles — a single scan may show apparent "scratches" that are actually light refraction
- Surface scoring should be more tolerant (+1.0 grade adjustment) for holo surface analysis
- Cosmos holo, reverse holo, and full art holo each have different baseline textures
- Only score genuine scratches that disrupt the holo pattern, not the pattern itself

## Scanner Resolution & Artifacts

You are grading from a 600 DPI flatbed scanner image, NOT a microscope or loupe. At this resolution:
- **Minor pixel noise, compression artifacts, and scan banding are NOT defects** — ignore them entirely
- **Holo refraction patterns** appear as colour shifts or apparent "scratches" — these are NOT physical damage
- **Factory card texture** (modern linen texture, etched holo) appears as a pattern in the scan — this is NOT surface damage
- **Dust specks on the scanner bed** may appear as tiny dark spots — do not count these as card defects
- A card that looks clean and sharp in a 600 DPI scan IS clean and sharp — do not imagine defects that aren't clearly visible
- **If you cannot clearly identify a specific physical defect, it is not a defect.** Do not penalise for vague impressions.
- **Zoom into corners carefully** — corner whitening is the most common defect and the easiest to miss in a scan. Even minor white fibres showing = 9.0 corners max.
- **Check surface under different contrast** — surface scratches can be subtle in scans but still affect the grade significantly. Any visible wear = 9.0 surface max.

## Grading Philosophy

RKT grading aims to be **fair but commercially viable**. We want to:
1. Grade accurately — the grade should reflect the card's true condition
2. **Avoid being unnecessarily harsh** — borderline calls should lean toward the higher grade
3. Be consistent — the same card should get the same grade every time
4. Prioritise what collectors care about — surface scratches on the artwork matter more than minor edge wear on the back
5. **Grade what you actually see** — if corners show whitening, score them accordingly even if the rest looks good

When in doubt between two grades, round UP to the next 0.5 increment if:
- The card has only minor defects
- The defects are on the back, not the front
- The card is vintage (pre-2003)
- Confidence in the defect detection is below 70%
- The "defect" could be a scanner artifact or holo refraction
